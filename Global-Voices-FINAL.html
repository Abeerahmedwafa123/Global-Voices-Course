About the Course

The Global Voices Speaking Course is an innovative online program that empowers learners to use their voices as tools for positive global change. Grounded in the IB framework and concept-based inquiry approach, this course integrates language mastery with meaningful global exploration. Each session invites learners to discuss real issues that matter — from poverty and equality to climate action and peace — while refining their communication, reasoning, and collaboration skills. Through dynamic dialogue, persuasive speaking, and storytelling, participants not only practice English fluency but also engage in deep inquiry about the world they live in.

Every 90-minute session aligns the IB Inquiry Cycle with the 5E instructional model (Engage → Explore → Explain → Elaborate → Evaluate) and a CELTA-informed text-based skills stage, climbing from understanding to debate, design and creation through Bloom's Taxonomy and Depth of Knowledge. Sessions run twice a week across nine thematic weeks, and each Goal is explored through its most natural IB transdisciplinary theme — by Session 17, all six themes have been covered. Every session closes with two non-negotiables: Make Conclusions and Take Action.
